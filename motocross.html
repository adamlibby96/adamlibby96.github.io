Pull up to the starting gate. Watch the person hold up the 30 second board. They tilt it sideways. They begin to run off the track. Only a few seconds till the gate drops. Engines are roaring. Take one last deep breath. Gate slams down. Let of the clutch and the race has begun.

One of the best feelings in the world is nailing the launch at the perfect time. All 30 of us trying to get through the first turn and making it through the carnage.
The thing I love most about motocross is the thrill and heart racing moments. I first began riding bikes when I was around 8 years old on my dad's old 50cc bike. After many years on the 50, I upgraded to a 65cc which was twice as fast.
Then came the big moment: riding my dad's Kawasaki KX125. Oh the sounds and power this bike has. I competed in my first race with this bike. I will never forget the first gate drop, first tripple, first table top. Everything about motocross is amazing in ways that are indescribable.
