Lost City of Atlantis

Assignment

Create an open, underwater enviornment using Unreal Engine 4. The theme: A Lost City.

Goals

Become fimilar with the Unreal Engine.
Challenge your creativity.
Add interactivity and flow to the scene.
Tell a story through your environment.
Create a focal point or point of interest.
Push yourself to learn other features.

My process

I started out with old fashion pencil and paper. I designed the layout of the map and then I started working on the terrain. On of the challenges that I encountered was just placing the art assets in way that looks natural. Once I started getting the hang of placing them in a realistic way, I started to work on the player controller. The biggest struggle here was working with UE4 blueprints. I started with the player's movement (forward/backward, left/right). Since this is an underwater level, the player's movement had to feel like they were actually swimming. So instead of just moving the character in the direction they press, I actually add a physics force in that direction, and since UE4 provides a water volume, the drag force was already applied to the player's movement.
After the movement I did the mouse look. I had two options: rotate the whole player, rotate the head joint. I went with the head joint because I wanted realist head movement. This was surprisingly easy. All I had to do was get a reference to the head joint object and then limit the rotation of it.
We were also required to have triggered events. I made 3 trigger events in my scene. The first one was when the player leaves the cave and it triggers a plane's physics to activate and it will fall to the ocean floor. Then my next one was when the player is about to enter the main building, a shark will swim out and hopefully scare the player a little. The last one is when the player picks up the golden knife it will trigger the end game cutscene.
