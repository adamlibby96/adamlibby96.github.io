Brief Description

    Hello! I'm Adam Libby. I have a love for technology, the great outdoors, and gaming. If I am not on my computer, I am either fishing or wood carving. I also have a passion for learning and helping people.







    I was born in Midland, Texas, then quickly moved to Houston. After spending several years there, my dad moved us to a small town called Bartlesville, Oklahoma. I have spent most of my life in Bartlesville. I have a fantastic family: Mom, Dad, and 3 brothers - 2 older, 1 younger. I followed my fathers footsteps and tried to race motorcycles, but school ended up taking over. I had an active childhood, especially with my 3 brothers in the house. We used to spend hours and hours outside. I am thankful that I have such an amazing and caring family.

    I attended high school in Bartlesville, OK. While I was there, I had the opportunity to attend a local trade school where I graduated with a certificate in Netwroking. I then moved on to college: Oklahoma Christian University (OC). Due to my time at tech school, I knew exactly what I wanted to do as a career: programming. I signed up for Computer Science at OC, and am now a senior majoring in Computer Science and minoring in Gaming and Animation. I intend to graduate in December of 2019.

    I discovered my love for programming during my senior year in highschool while attending a technical college for networking. My professor gave us two weeks to complete a small program in Java. This lead me to creating a replica of Snake. Obviously being my first time programming, it was the buggiest code I have ever seen. But nonetheless, I loved starting with an empty file and writing some lines of code and watching the project come alive.

    Studying here at OC I have discovered that I really enjoy programming in Unity3d with C#. I started out with making a game based off an old TV series 'American Gladiators.' I have recently found the love for developing VR games.

    I enjoy going camping, riding motorcycles, and taking my /*derpy*/ husky, Luna, on runs. I received my love for camping from my Scouting Career. I also picked up racing motorcycles from my father. This past summer my father took me fishing and I instantly fell inlove with it. I also enjoy wood carving, especially around a campfire.

    I was elected Crew Chief for a high-adventure camp called Philmont. As Crew Chief I led a twelve-man crew on a 111-mile backpacking trek. That trip was easily one of my greatest accomplishments. After Philmont, I put together my Eagle project at our local ASPCA. Our job was to clean out their backyard/junkyard, mow and weed eat, and build a few shelves for storage. My team and I put well over 150 man-hours completing this project. I achieved my Eagle Scout rank in 2014.
